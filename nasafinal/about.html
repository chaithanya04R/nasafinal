What are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. These distant worlds come in many forms, from giant gas planets larger than Jupiter to rocky worlds like Earth. Some exoplanets orbit their stars so closely that they are much hotter than any planet in our solar system.

How Are Exoplanets Discovered?

Most exoplanets are found by observing the light of stars. When a planet crosses in front of its star, there is a tiny dip in brightness, called a transit. The most successful method for discovering exoplanets has been through space telescopes such as Kepler and TESS.
